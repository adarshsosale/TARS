A self-contained snapshot of the Growbot 3D-redesign task so work can resume cold.

## What Growbot is
A real, buildable bipedal robot inspired by Interstellar's **TARS** — but **not** the
4-panel "dismantle" toy. Three vertical rectangular slabs (faithful "big rectangle +
2 small rectangle legs"):
- **Central torso slab** = battery + electronics enclosure, and a slightly-raised
  **rest foot** (tripod / anti-tip support).
- **Two outer leg slabs** = the walking legs, each with hip + ankle pitch joints and a
  TPU-soled foot.
Goal of the current phase: an **MVP to validate "full motion and form"** — NOT
load-bearing, NOT lifting. Will upgrade servos later (the MG996Rs get reused in an RC car).

## Coordinate system & key dimensions (mm)
- **+Y up**, **+Z forward** (walking dir), **±X lateral** (= the joint pitch axis).
- Standing height **290 (~11.4 in)** — shortened from 305 to offset the wider torso and
  hold mass ~constant. Chassis = hollow shells, **WALL = 3 mm**. `SPLIT_Y = 150` (bed seam).
- Torso `T_W 82 × T_D 58` (W 76→82 so the 2 hip servos go **coaxial**), slab Y 20→290.
  Legs `L_W 44 × L_D 44` (W 38→44 for the ankle servo), centers at **X=±69**. Leg shell
  **Y 52→290**; **Y 34→52 = the ankle gap**. `GAP 6` torso↔leg. Hip pivot **Y=230**. Feet
  `55×95`, Y 6→34, `PAD_H 6`. Central rest foot `60×50`, sole raised `CF_CLEAR 8` mm.
  Overall footprint ~**193 (X) × 97 (Z)**.
- Panel aesthetic (the TARS look, all real relief): **uniform vertical columns
  (`PANEL_COL 9.5`) + VARYING-height rows** (`ROW_UNIT 5`, `ROW_PATTERN
  [2,3,2,4,2,3,3,2,4,2]`, groove 1.1, relief 0.5). Gridded top end-caps (`CAP_TGT 9`),
  ribbed dark grip bands, nameplate placard + 5 indicator dots, treaded TPU soles.

## Drivetrain — DIRECT-DRIVE SERVOS (final decision)
4 powered DOF: **2 hip-pitch + 2 ankle-pitch**, all about the **X axis**.
- **Direct drive with hobby servos — no worm, no external gears.** (An earlier
  worm-gear design was dropped: it was over-engineered once we re-estimated the robot
  as light ~1.4 kg with low torque needs. The servo's internal gearbox is the reduction.)
- **Hip servos — ×2, ONE PER LEG, COAXIAL & SYMMETRIC**: MG996R in the torso, back-to-back
  on the **same X axis at Z=0**, spline + horn out to each leg at X=±43, Y=230. The torso
  was widened **76→82 mm** to fit them coaxially (interior 76 ≥ 2×37). This replaces the
  old ±12 mm Z stagger — symmetric load + symmetric motors = **no fore/aft asymmetry for
  the control layer to compensate**. Both visible in `section_hip_top.png` / `hip_3d.png`.
- **Ankle servos**: MG996R **standing in each lower leg** (shaft on X), driving a
  **crank → pushrod → foot rocker** (a 4-bar). The foot pitches about a real **hinge
  pin** (ankle axis X, Y≈43); Y 34→52 under the leg is the open ankle gap. The leg was
  **widened 38→44 mm** so the 37 mm-long servo body fits *inside* it — it can't fit a
  38 mm leg, which is exactly why the old design buried it in the wide (55 mm) foot.
  Rocker:crank ≈ 16.6:13.5 → ankle torque ≈ **1.2× the servo** with ±~49° foot travel
  (both tunable via the crank/rocker radii). Pushrod runs **outboard** (exposed/
  serviceable — add a cover for a real build).
- **Central rest foot** (`CF_CLEAR=8`) is the parking/anti-tip support; set `CF_CLEAR=0`
  for a permanent tripod (gives up walking).

## Mass / balance (now auto-computed — generator prints the breakdown each run)
- **1.40 kg** at 3 mm walls (≈**1.15 kg** at 2 mm). Held ~constant through the coaxial-hip
  rework: torso widening +height-shorten (305→290) ≈ a wash (that was the whole point).
- Breakdown (g): printed ABS shell+panels+bosses **808** · printed CF drive **11** · TPU
  **98** · 4× MG996R **220** · LiPo 194 · electronics 106 → **1401**. (1.42→1.44 widening,
  then −shorten back to 1.40.)
- **CoM at X≈0, Y≈124 mm (41% height), Z≈0** — **below the hip (Y=230)** → pendulum-stable.
  (CoM not yet recomputed for the new ankle; ankle mass moved up into the leg, so the real
  CoM is slightly **higher** than 124 — still well below the hip.)

## Drive ratios (the "gear ratios")
- **Hip = the main drive = DIRECT, ratio 1:1.** The MG996R's own gearbox (~1:270 internal)
  is the only reduction. Available ~**11 kgf·cm @6 V**. Demand: free-leg swing ≈ 1.5 kgf·cm;
  worst single-stance body lean (20°, ~1.15 kg above the hip, h≈106 mm) ≈ **4.2 kgf·cm** →
  **~2× margin**. Fine for the slow MVP. *Dynamic walking wants a reduction* → either a
  printed **2:1** stage or a stronger servo (DS3225 ~25 kgf·cm) — deferred.
- **Ankle = pushrod, ratio ≈ 1.23:1 (torque advantage)** from rocker:crank = 16.6:13.5.
  → ~13.5 kgf·cm before friction (≈11–12 after) vs ~6.7 kgf·cm worst case → **~1.8× margin**.
  Trade: foot sweep = servo sweep ÷ 1.23 (≈ ±49° available). Tune via the crank/rocker radii.

## Wiring (see `wiring.png`) — rerouted so it no longer passes through any solid part
- Each **ankle servo's 3-wire lead** (V+/GND/signal) runs **up the leg cavity at Z≈+15**
  (behind the servo, which is Z≤10), **crosses ABOVE the hip servos** (Y≈248 > the servo
  tops at 245) with a **service loop**, then **down the torso side wall** (clear of the
  hips/bucks) and **in to the PCA9685** through the gap above the battery. The old path
  speared the hip servo body — fixed. Drill ~6 mm grommet holes at the leg↔torso crossing;
  strain-relief the loop. **Hip servo leads are short** (servo sits by the board).

## Print splits & screw joints (`screws_torso`, `screws_leg_L/R`)
- Tall slabs are bed-split at **`SPLIT_Y = 150`** (inside the dark accent band → hides the
  seam). Each split is bolted: **printed bosses straddle the seam** (lower half = M3
  heat-set insert, upper half = clearance hole) + an **M3 screw**. Torso = **4 bosses**
  at the interior corners; each leg = **4 bosses**. Gusset the bosses to the wall corners
  in CAD; print upper/lower halves separately and bolt. See `print_parts_3d.png` (halves
  lifted apart, every part colour-coded by what prints as one piece).
- Power tree unchanged: PCA9685 V+ ← **XL4016 @6 V**; Pi ← **MP1584 @5 V**; **common GND**;
  1000 µF on the 6 V rail. Modelled as `o` groups `wiring_L/R`.

## Hardware / BOM (decided)
- **Servos:** 4× TowerPro **MG996R** (55 g, ~11 kgf·cm @6 V). *(Upgrade path if it
  validates: DS3225 coreless ~₹1.6k, or Waveshare ST3215 smart-bus ~₹2.2k.)*
- **Brain:** **Raspberry Pi Zero 2W** (have) + microSD (have). NOT the ESP32 (also owned;
  could be a real-time servo/IMU co-processor later if Linux jitter bites).
- **GPIO header:** 2×20 **male, through-hole, 2.54 mm** (Zero 2W ships bare — must solder).
- **Servo driver:** **PCA9685** (essential — the Pi can't cleanly PWM 4 servos).
- **IMU:** **MPU-6050** (have 2; use 1). I2C addr 0x68. **No magnetometer** → no EMI worry
  but yaw drifts (fine: only pitch/roll needed for balance). **Mount at CoM (0, ~124, 0)**,
  axes aligned to body, on a vibration-damped bracket.
- **Power:** **2S 7.4 V 3300 mAh LiPo** → **XL4016 8 A buck set to 6.0 V** for the 4 servos;
  **MP1584 buck @5.0–5.1 V** for the Pi. **1000 µF cap** on the 6 V servo rail.
  - Wiring: `LiPo → XL4016(6V) → PCA9685 V+ → servos` ; `LiPo → MP1584(5V) → Pi 5V pin` ;
    `Pi I2C(3.3V) → PCA9685(0x40) + MPU-6050(0x68)` ; **all grounds common**.
  - Gotchas: **set the XL4016 to 6.0 V with a multimeter BEFORE connecting servos**
    (ships high → can fry them); never power servos off the Pi; separate rails + the cap
    prevent servo-spike brownout resets. PCA9685 has two supplies — VCC (3.3 V logic from Pi)
    vs V+ (6 V servo power) — don't cross them.
- **Charger:** iMAX **B6** (DC-input 11–18 V) — needs a separate **12 V ≥5 A adapter,
  5.5×2.1 mm, center-positive** (likely not bundled).
- **Now required (designed in):** M3 heat-set inserts + M3 screws for the bed-split seams
  (torso 4, each leg 4) and the joints. **Still deferred:** wires/XT60 lead, power switch,
  optional joint pivot bearings (623/625) if you upgrade the printed pins to steel.
